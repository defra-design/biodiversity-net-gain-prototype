You can use this service to:

  • register land in England so it can be used as a biodiversity gain site
  • record the biodiversity gains a development project will make on another piece of land (known as 'off-site' biodiversity gains)
  • register land and record off-site biodiversity gains at the same time
  • check the status of your registrations or records

Registering land as a biodiversity gain site

You'll need:

  • a Defra account
  • consent from the landowner or leaseholder
  • a legal agreement that secures the site for at least 30 years
  • a local land charge search certificate if using a conservation covenant
  • proof of ownership of the land
  • a map showing the boundary of the biodiversity gain site
  • a completed Biodiversity Metric 4.0
  • a habitat management and monitoring plan

Recording off-site biodiversity gains for a development project

You'll need:

  • a Defra account
  • the Biodiversity Metric 4.0 file that your local planning authority reviewed with your Biodiversity Net Gain (BNG) Statement
  • consent from biodiversity gain site owners

Registering land and recording off-site biodiversity gains at the same time

You'll need a development project's Biodiversity Metric 4.0 file, as well as everything you need to register land as a biodiversity gain site. It must be the Biodiversity Metric 4.0 file a local planning authority reviewed with the development project's BNG Statement.
