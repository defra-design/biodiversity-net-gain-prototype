A registered biodiversity gain site is land that development projects can use for off-site gain. These off-site gains contribute to the minimum 10% biodiversity net gain (BNG) that development projects need.

Anyone who owns or leases land can register a biodiversity gain site. Someone can do it on the landowner's behalf if they have their consent.

What you need to register

You need:

  • consent from the landowner or leaseholder
  • a legal agreement that secures the site for at least 30 years
  • a local land charge search certificate if using a conservation covenant
  • proof of ownership of the land
  • a map showing the boundary of the biodiversity gain site
  • a completed Biodiversity Metric 4.0
  • a habitat management and monitoring plan

Recording habitats being used for off-site gain when you register

You can record habitats being used for off-site gain by a development project at the same as registering a biodiversity gain site. To do this you'll need a development project's Biodiversity Metric 4.0 file. It must be the Metric file a local planning authority reviewed with the development project's BNG Statement.
