Overview

You need to register land that developers can use for biodiversity gain if they cannot use their development site.

The developer needs this registration to get their biodiversity gain plan approved before development works begin.

Before you apply

If the land is linked to a development

You must have a legal agreement called a conservation covenant or a planning obligation

The developer must submit a planning application using the Planning portal

If the land is not yet linked to a development

You must have a conservation covenant.

Check what you need and apply

You'll need to have:

  • consent from the landowner or third parties who have an interest in the land
  • a copy of the signed conservation covenant or a planning portal reference number
  • the boundary of the land
  • your completed biodiversity metric 3.0 calculations
  • your habitat management and monitoring plan

It costs £? to apply. You can pay by debit or credit card or Bacs.

Other ways to apply

You can also apply by post using form BNG01.

Postal applications take 10 days and cost £? on top of the application fee.

Send your application to the address on the form.

After you apply

After you submit your application, it can take Natural England up to 28 days to review your application.

We will:

  • give you a site reference number
  • check the information you've provided

If your application is approved

Only land that is linked to a development will be listed on the Biodiversity gain site register.

After you're registered

You must follow the habitat management and monitoring plan.

You must tell Natural England if:

  • the landowner changes
  • a developer can no longer operate
  • there are changes to the planning permission

If the whole land or a part of it becomes linked to a new development

You must add the development to your registration.
