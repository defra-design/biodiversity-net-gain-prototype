The developer needs this registration before development works can begin.

Consultants can apply on behalf of developers or landowners.

Before you apply

Planning application

The developer needs to submit a planning application using the Planning portal.

As part of their planning application, they need to include a draft or final biodiversity gain plan.

The draft or final biodiversity gain plan needs to include:

  • completed biodiversity metric 3.0 calculations for the land being used by the development
  • draft or final habitat or management monitoring plan
  • site reference number from this registration

The Local Planning Authority could give planning permission with drafts of the plans. However, the developer will need to get approval of the final plans before they can begin development works.

Legal agreements

You need legal agreements that secure funds from a developer to pay for the biodiversity gain for 30 years or more.

You can use a signed agreement called a planning obligation, which is between a landowner and a developer.

If you do not have a planning obligation, you need 2 other signed agreements:

  • a conservation covenant that secures the biodiversity gain for 30 years or more
  • a financial agreement that secures funding from a developer for the biodiversity gain

Interested third parties

You need to confirm with all interested third parties that your application does not conflict with their interests.

A third party can be someone who has shooting rights, public right of way or is the mortage lender.

Information from the landowner

You also need the following from the landowner:

  • signed consent to register the land
  • a copy of the certificate of ownership for the land
  • the boundary of the land
  • completed biodiversity metric 3.0 calculations for the whole land

Apply

It costs £? to apply. You can pay by debit or credit card or Bacs.

Other ways to apply

You can also apply by post using form BNG01.

Postal applications take 10 days and cost £? on top of the application fee.

Send your application to the address on the form.

After you apply

After you submit your application, it can take Natural England up to 28 days to review your application.

We will:

  • give you a site reference number
  • check the information you've provided

If your application is approved

The land will be listed on the Biodiversity gain site register.

After you're registered

You must follow your habitat management and monitoring plan.

You must tell Natural England if:

  • the landowner changes
  • a developer can no longer operate
  • there are changes to the planning permission
