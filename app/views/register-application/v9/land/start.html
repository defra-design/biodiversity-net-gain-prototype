If you're the consultant you can register on behalf of landowners.

Before you register

Before you register please check you have the following:

  • signed consent from the landowner to register the land
  • a copy of the certificate of ownership, if the land is not registered with HM Land Registry
  • the boundary of the land being used for biodiversity gain
  • details of the available habitat enhancements, including habitat type, area or length and condition
  • the habitat or management monitoring plan

Interested third parties

You need to confirm with all interested third parties that your registration does not conflict with their interests.

Legal agreements

You need legal agreements that secure funds from a developer to pay for the biodiversity gain for 30 years or more.

You can use a signed agreement called a planning obligation, which is between a landowner and a developer.

If you do not have a planning obligation, you need 2 other signed agreements:

  • a conservation covenant that secures the biodiversity gain for 30 years or more
  • a financial agreement that secures funding from a developer for the biodiversity gain

Registration fee

It costs £240 to register. You can pay by debit or credit card.
