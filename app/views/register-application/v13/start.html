Register a biodiversity gain site

Use this service to register a biodiversity gain site in England. Once registered, developers will be able to use it as off-site land to meet at least 10% biodiversity net gain.

Who can register

A landowner or leaseholder can register a biodiversity gain site. Someone acting on behalf of the landowner or leaseholder can register the site as long as they have consent.

What you'll need

To register a biodiversity gain site, you'll need:

  • consent from the landowner or leaseholder - if needed
  • a planning obligation (S106 agreement) or conservation covenant securing the site for at least 30 years
  • proof of ownership of the land
  • a map showing the boundary of the land
  • a completed Biodiversity Metric 4.0
  • a habitat management and monitoring plan

When you start, we'll check that you have everything that you need to register a biodiversity gain site. You'll be able to skip these checks if you want.
