Registering a biodiversity gain site

Register land as biodiversity gain sites in England. Once registered, the biodiversity units on the gain site can be used by development projects which need to meet at least 10% biodiversity net gain.

Anyone who owns or leases land can register a biodiversity gain site. Someone can do it on their behalf if they have consent to register.

What you'll need to register a biodiversity gain site

You'll need:

  • consent from the landowner or leaseholder - if needed
  • a planning obligation (S106 agreement) or conservation covenant securing the site for at least 30 years
  • proof of ownership of the land
  • a map showing the boundary of the biodiversity gain site
  • a completed Biodiversity Metric 4.0
  • a habitat management and monitoring plan

Recording off-site biodiversity gains for a development project

By law, you have to record any off-site land you plan to use for biodiversity net gain with your development project. You'll need to meet at least a 10% biodiversity net gain. Local planning authorities will set the exact percentage of net gain needed.

You will not be able to discharge a BNG pre-commencement planning condition with a local planning authority until this has been done.

What you'll need to record off-site biodiversity gains for a development project

You'll need the Biodiversity 4.0 Metric file that was approved by the local planning authority. It should include:

  • names of the development projects
  • planning references
  • local authority they're in
  • area and length of the habitats the development projects are using on gain sites

You'll also need:

  • the biodiversity gain site number for the off-site
  • to have consent from landowners for any gain sites you're using
