Use this service to record biodiversity gains in England.

You can:

  • register a biodiversity gain site
  • record off-site biodiversity gains for a development project

Register a biodiversity gain site

Register biodiversity gain sites in England. The biodiversity units on the gain site can be used by development projects which need to meet at least 10% biodiversity net gain.

Anyone who owns or leases land can register a biodiversity gain site. Someone can do it on the landowner's behalf if they have their consent to register.

What you'll need to register a biodiversity gain site

You'll need:

  • consent from the landowner or leaseholder - if needed
  • a planning obligation (S106 agreement) or conservation covenant securing the site for at least 30 years
  • proof of ownership of the land
  • a map showing the boundary of the biodiversity gain site
  • a completed Biodiversity Metric 4.0
  • a habitat management and monitoring plan

To record biodiversity gains at the same time as registering a biodiversity gain site, you'll need the:

  • planning references
  • local planning authority for the development projects
  • area and length of the habitats the development projects are using on the biodiversity gain sites

Once we have checked the biodiversity gain site and development project details we will add them to the public register.

Record off-site biodiversity gains for a development project

By law, you have to record any off-site biodiversity gains you plan to use for your development project. You'll need to meet at least a 10% biodiversity net gain. Local planning authorities will set the exact percentage of net gain needed.

You will not be able to discharge a BNG pre-commencement planning condition with a local planning authority until this has been done.
