Use this service to record biodiversity gains in England.

You can:

  • register a biodiversity gain site
  • record off-site biodiversity gains for a development project

Register a biodiversity gain site

Register biodiversity gain sites in England. A registered biodiversity gain site is land that development projects can use for their off-site gain. These off-site gains contribute to the minimum 10% biodiversity net gain (BNG) needed by development projects.

Anyone who owns or leases land can register a biodiversity gain site. Someone can do it on the landowner's behalf if they have their consent to register.

What you'll need to register a biodiversity gain site

You'll need:

  • consent from the landowner or leaseholder
  • a conservation covenant that secures the site for at least 30 years
  • proof of ownership of the land
  • a map showing the boundary of the biodiversity gain site
  • a completed Biodiversity Metric 4.0
  • a habitat management and monitoring plan

We will check that the biodiversity gain site details submitted are all correct. If everything is correct we will add it to the BNG public register.

Record off-site biodiversity gains for a development project

Development projects are legally required to use this service to record off-site gains. Any new development will need to meet at least a 10% biodiversity net gain.

We will check the information you record. This is to avoid allocating the same habitat on a biodiversity gain site to multiple developers. You'll need to have written consent from the biodiversity gain site owners.

Local planning authorities will not discharge a BNG pre-commencement planning condition until the off-site gains have been recorded.

What you'll need to record off-site biodiversity gains for a development project

If you're using habitat on a registered biodiversity gain site. You'll need:

  • the Biodiversity Metric 4.0 file that your local planning authority reviewed with your Biodiversity Net Gain Statement
  • to have written consent from the biodiversity gain site owners
