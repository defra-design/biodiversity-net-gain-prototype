Use this service to record biodiversity gains in England.

You can:

  • register a biodiversity gain site
  • record off-site biodiversity gains for a development project

Register a biodiversity gain site

Register biodiversity gain sites in England. Once registered, development projects will be able to use it as off-site land to meet at least 10% biodiversity net gain.

Anyone who owns or leases land can register a biodiversity gain site. Someone can do it on the landowner's behalf if they have their consent to register.

What you'll need to register a biodiversity gain site

You'll need:

  • consent from the landowner or leaseholder - if needed
  • conservation covenant securing the site for at least 30 years
  • proof of ownership of the land
  • a map showing the boundary of the biodiversity gain site
  • a completed Biodiversity Metric 4.0
  • a habitat management and monitoring plan

Once we have checked the biodiversity gain site details we will add them to the public register.

Record off-site biodiversity gains for a development project

By law, development projects have to record any off-site biodiversity gains they plan to use. Development projects need to meet at least a 10% biodiversity net gain. Local planning authorities will set the exact percentage of net gain needed.

Local planning authorities will not discharge the BNG pre-commencement planning conditions until the off-site gains have been recorded.

What you'll need to record off-site biodiversity gains for a development project

If you're using habitat on a registered biodiversity gain site. You'll need:

  • the Biodiversity Metric 4.0 file that was approved by the local planning authoritys
  • the biodiversity gain site number for the off-site
  • to have consent from landowners for any gain sites you're using
