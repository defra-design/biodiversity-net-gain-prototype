You'll need:

  • the title deeds or lease agreement to prove ownership
  • written authorisation from the landowner or leaseholder if you're applying on their behalf
  • a map showing the land boundary
  • a completed Biodiversity Metric 4.0
  • a habitat management and monitoring plan (this may be within your legal agreement)
  • a planning obligation (Section 106) or conservation covenant that secures the site for at least 30 years
  • a local land charge search certificate

Why your application could be rejected

The land you tell us about could be rejected as a biodiversity gain site if:

  • it's already registered under the same legal agreement
  • you do not pay the fee within 28 days of making the application
  • you do not provide any additional information we might ask for after making the application
